As We May Think

I read a New York Times article named "The Secret History of Women in Coding," and learned many things. I thought about this when the author, Vannevar Bush, mentioned Babbage and not Ada Lovelace. As well as when he mentioned that there will be a "whole roomful of girls" delivering instructions and data to the machines. This reminded me of how the article mentioned in the beginning there were only women in computing. Another thing that striked me was that I don't think anybody could have predicted what was to come out next. Specifically, how far technology would have actually advanced.


I think the thing that scared me the most was reading that ISPs could potentially limit the websites you can use. They could ultimately charge you different prices for different "packages." The packages being the websites you could have access to. As Tim Berners-Lee suggested that would compromise the promise of universitality the Web is supposed to have kept.
